# Campaign Page — Missing / To Do

1. **Pricing** — The campaign table lists what's available but has no prices. Even indicative or "starting from" numbers would help.

2. **Timeline and delivery** — When do backers expect to receive their boards? A simple table of milestones (hardware finalisation, manufacturing, shipping) would cover this.

3. **Open source status** — Is the hardware design open? Are schematics and KiCad files going to be published? Is the firmware MIT/Apache/GPL? A significant trust signal on CrowdSupply. Even "design files will be published on GitHub after the campaign" is worth stating.

4. **Getting started — what does the first 10 minutes look like?** — A short step-by-step walkthrough (install lumos, create a project, build, flash) would make the "no setup friction" promise concrete and believable.

5. **Third-party shield compatibility** — Is the mezzanine connector spec published? Can someone design their own compatible shield? If the connector is open, the ecosystem is not limited to what you personally build. This would dramatically strengthen the ecosystem story.

6. **Risks and challenges** — CrowdSupply campaigns are expected to address this honestly. Supply chain, component availability (STM32 lead times in particular have been volatile), manufacturing risks. A short, candid paragraph builds trust rather than undermining it.

7. **Community and support** — Where do buyers go for help? GitHub, Discord, a forum? Is there a place to share projects? Especially important for a technical product where people will have firmware questions.
